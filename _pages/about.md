Hello! I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Linguistics at the University of Toronto. My research centres on the documentation, revitalization, and reclamation of the Oneida language.

My dissertation investigates the phonetics of Oneida and the phonetic acquisition of adult learners. Through acoustic analyses of native speakers’ speech, I examine the Oneida vowels, word-level prosody, and oral stops. I am also studying how adult second-language learners acquire the oral stops, contributing to the broader discussions of L2 phonetic acquisition in the Indigenous language revitalization context.

Guided by a community-based approach, I work in partnership with the Twatati Oneida Revitalization Project of the Oneida Nation of the Thames community to ensure that the research outcomes are meaningful and accessible. Ultimately, I aim to transform the findings of this research into pedagogical materials that support sustaining the ongoing legacy of the Oneida language.
